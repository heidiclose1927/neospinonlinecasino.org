4. Recognizing Problem Gambling

Problem gambling can have severe consequences for individuals and their families. While most people gamble responsibly, it's important to be aware of the signs of problem gambling. These may include:

  • Spending more money and time gambling than you can afford.
  • Chasing losses, trying to win back money you've lost.
  • Gambling affecting your work, relationships, or financial situation.
  • Feeling restless or irritable when trying to cut down or stop gambling.
  • Hiding the extent of your gambling from others.
  • Borrowing money to gamble or to pay gambling debts.

5. Seeking Help for Problem Gambling

If you or someone you know is struggling with problem gambling, we strongly encourage seeking professional help. There are many organizations dedicated to providing support and guidance:

  • Gamblers Anonymous: A fellowship of men and women who share their experience, strength and hope with each other that they may solve their common problem and help others to recover from a gambling problem. (www.gamblersanonymous.org)
  • National Council on Problem Gambling (NCPG): Provides a helpline and resources for those affected by problem gambling in the USA. (www.ncpgambling.org)
  • GamCare: A leading provider of information, advice, and support for anyone affected by problem gambling in the UK. (www.gamcare.org.uk)
  • Responsible Gambling Council (RGC): An independent non-profit organization dedicated to problem gambling prevention. (www.responsiblegambling.org)
